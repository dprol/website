Carpooling

Flexibility is important in any sort of long-term commitment, and this class is no exception. In order to help you work out logistics for getting to class and back, I have put together a class roster with location and contact information for those who have given me permission to do so.

You can use this roster to communicate and arrange carpooling with other families at your convenience. However, the requirement that at least one parent must attend the first session in the fall class and, if applicable, the first session in the spring class, still holds.

TeenCoder™ Purchase

For the first class session on August 18, your student will need to bring their laptop and all of their TeenCoder™ materials (physical textbook, plus all digital materials). Therefore, you must purchase TeenCoder™ as soon as possible so that you will have those materials before class starts. You can purchase TeenCoder™ here.

Since it contains exactly the TeenCoder™ materials we will need in this class, I would strongly recommend purchasing the TeenCoder™: Java Programming Complete Course bundle, which is $75 and can be found between The Year Pack and TeenCoder™: Android Programming bundles on the page I've linked. If you would like, however, you can purchase a different bundle, but the bundle that you purchase must at least include the TeenCoder™: Java Programming Complete Course materials.

Snacks

Programmers need their snacks! We'd like to ask each family to bring snacks three times during the fall semester. More detailed information is available on the snack signup sheet.

Plan to bring snacks for at least 10 people. Nothing really fancy is necessary; a simple snack is perfectly fine. What exactly you bring is up to you, but there are some snack ideas on the spreadsheet just in case.

Medical Release Form

Unless you plan to stay with your student, please be sure to bring a completed medical release form to the first class session if you have not already sent it to me.

Inclement Weather

Please make an effort to attend every class session and to arrive a few minutes early so that instruction can begin promptly at 9:00 AM. If we have inclement weather, we will cancel by email by 7:00 AM that day and try to make the class up later in the week if possible. If a class is canceled and cannot be made up, we will electronically provide you with the slides and homework assignments for that week. Even if class is cancelled, due dates for homework assignments do not change.

If it snows, but we decide not to cancel class, there are a couple things to keep in mind about parking in our driveway. Only the paved part of the driveway will be plowed, so driving around during drop-off or pick-up may be tight. Finally, be sure not to drive or park near the roof if snow is on it; because of the solar panels, there is a very real hazard of snow sliding off and damaging your car or hurting someone.
